What is a meteorite?



You may know them as a shooting or falling star from the sky⁠—they form a fireball, during it's passage through the atmosphere, to reach the surface of a planet. In the simplest terms, a meteorite is a rock that falls to Earth from space. They are usually debris from an object, such as a comet, asteroid, or meteroid. They vary greatly in size!

This is a project about meteorite landings throughout the world. The data includes the following: name, id, nametype, recclass (material of meteorite), mass (in g), fall (recorded as fell or founded), year (recorded), reclat, and reclong. The data will feature meteorites that weigh, at and over, 10kg (~20lbs). Additionally, the top five heaviest meteorites will be highlighted.

Source of information is from NASA Open Data Portal and Astromaterials Research & Exploration Science.
